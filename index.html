The main difference — why every competitor fails the same way

Three gaps no competitor fills

1
Every competitor requires a login. Float App, Dryrun, Cash Flow Frog, Fathom, PlanGuru, Centime, QuickBooks Planner — every single one is a dashboard you must remember to open. The evidence from their own churn data is clear: SMB owners running 60–70 hour weeks do not check financial dashboards. The entire Category B competitor set has the same fatal design flaw. Engagement rates are low, churn is high, and the reason is almost always "I stopped looking at it." Pulse changes the model: the insight finds the owner, not the other way around.
2
Nobody built for Canada. Not one competitor — including Mastercard's virtual CFO in development — offers proactive GST/HST reserve management for Canadian SMBs. Not one integrates with ADP Run for Canadian payroll. The cash flow forecasting category was built in Edinburgh (Float), Edmonton moving upmarket (Dryrun), Australia (Fathom), and the US (PlanGuru, Cash Flow Frog, Centime). All have generic tax support or none. Canada has 5% GST federally and 13–15% HST provincially, with quarterly filing deadlines that blindside SMB owners every year. Zero competitors address this.
3
They surface numbers. Pulse surfaces actions. Even the best dashboard tools tell you what the forecast looks like. None of them send a message that says: "You'll miss payroll in 9 days. Here's the one invoice to chase — tap to send a reminder." The gap is execution, not information. Every competitor stops at the dashboard. Pulse extends into one-tap action: invoice reminders, payroll alerts, GST reserve warnings — all delivered before the owner opens their restaurant, before the job site starts, before the inbox fills up.
